Preventive Care

Empower women to understand and access preventive healthcare services in the U.S.

Key Learning Points

  • Preventive care means visiting the doctor before you feel sick.
  • Regular screenings and vaccines protect you and your family.
  • Insurance often covers preventive care at no extra cost.

Content

  • Primary Care Provider (PCP): A doctor or nurse you see for yearly check-ups.
  • Screenings:
    • Pap test: cervical cancer (every 3 years, ages 21–65)
    • Mammogram: breast cancer (every 1–2 years, ages 40–74)
    • Blood pressure, cholesterol, diabetes checks
  • Vaccines: Flu shot every year, Tdap, COVID-19 boosters
  • Why it matters: Preventive care helps catch problems early

Practical Tips

  • Write down your family medical history to share with your doctor.
  • Use a calendar or phone reminders for check-ups.
