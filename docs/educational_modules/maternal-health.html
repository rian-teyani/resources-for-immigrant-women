Maternal Health

Supporting women through pregnancy, childbirth, and postpartum care.

Maternal health focuses on the well-being of women during pregnancy, childbirth, and the postpartum period. Proper care ensures healthier outcomes for both mother and baby.

Key Points

  • Prenatal care protects both mother and baby.
  • Nutrition, rest, and medical check-ups are essential.
  • Postpartum care is as important as pregnancy care.

During Pregnancy

  • Visit a doctor or midwife as soon as you know you’re pregnant.
  • Take folic acid supplements to support healthy baby development.
  • Avoid alcohol, smoking, and certain medications.

Childbirth

  • Create a birth plan detailing support people and pain relief options.
  • Hospitals and birthing centers offer different services—ask what is available.

After Birth

  • Attend checkups for both mother and baby.
  • Watch for signs of postpartum depression, such as sadness, fatigue, or hopelessness.
  • Seek breastfeeding support and explore family planning options.

Practical Tips

  • Ask for interpreter services at clinics—they are free by law.
  • Bring a trusted support person to appointments.
