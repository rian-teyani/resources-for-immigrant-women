Navigating the U.S. Healthcare System

Understand how to access and use healthcare services in the U.S.

The U.S. healthcare system can be complex, but understanding the types of care, insurance options, and patient rights helps you access the care you need.

Key Points

  • Healthcare involves doctors, nurses, clinics, and hospitals.
  • Insurance helps pay for care, but free or low-cost options exist.
  • Patients have rights, including language access.

Types of Care

Primary Care
Regular doctor visits for check-ups, preventive care, and ongoing health management. Your primary care provider is your first point of contact for health concerns.
Emergency Care
For life-threatening situations, call 911 or go to the nearest emergency department immediately.
Urgent Care
For non-life-threatening conditions that require quick attention, such as minor injuries or infections. Urgent care clinics are faster than the ER for these issues.

Insurance Basics

Medicaid
Free or low-cost health insurance for low-income families. Eligibility varies by state.
ACA Marketplace
Affordable insurance plans under the Affordable Care Act. Check enrollment periods and subsidies that may reduce costs.

Patient Rights

Interpreter Services
Free language interpretation is available at healthcare facilities by law. Always ask if you need help understanding your care.
Medical Records
You have the right to request and receive copies of your medical records.
Clear Explanations
Providers must give clear explanations about your health, treatment options, and medications.

Practical Tips

  • Carry a list of medications and allergies to every visit.
  • Use community health workers or navigators for guidance when needed.
